Project Overview

Project Objective: Work alongside CoreLogic to design and build a widget that enables real estate investors to peruse CoreLogic’s massive database of properties available for purchase in the US.
Role: Head UI/UX designer & front end developer
Tools/Technologies Used: Figma, wireframe.cc, Xtensio, Photoshop, Angular, CoreLogic APIs, GitHub
Course: Senior Capstone Project
Partner: CoreLogic
Project Timeline: October 2018 - March 2019

The task at hand

For my senior capstone project, myself and 4 other students were paired with an Irvine based company named CoreLogic. CoreLogic is an Irvine based real estate data company that has been collecting data on 99% of the properties in the United States over that last 50 or so years. As of now their main business comes from selling information on properties and conducting real estate appraisals.

Now they are looking to make use of their massive amount of data in a new way by developing a tool that allows real estate investors, both casual and professional, to search for properties for sale throughout the entire US. This is where we come in.

Competitive Analysis

Before we started designing anything it was important to get a lay of the land as far as real estate software goes. So we conducted a competitive analysis of some of our main competitors: RoofStock, HomeUnion, and HouseCanary.


RoofStock

Roofstock allows casual investors to search for, analyze, and buy single-family homes with tenants already in them as a way to make passive income. It is a service that allows investors to focus on “asset allocation” (such as in the stock market), rather than researching and buying vacant homes that need to be repaired and leased. Roofstock is a competitor that our partners at CoreLogic think does a solid job at allowing users to easily find and manage properties. However, Roofstock only operates in 16 states whereas CoreLogic already has information on the majority of properties in the entire United States.


HomeUnion

HomeUnion is an online real estate investment management firm that helps “small-time investors” buy, renovate, and rent single-family rental properties. HomeUnion operates along the full lifecycle: locating properties, advising on the purchase, finding renters, managing the property, and selling. HomeUnion directly manages property, rather than refer buyers to property managers (Roofstock).


HouseCanary

HouseCanary leverages comprehensive data and predictive analytics to help investors make more informed real estate decisions. Utilizing more than four decades of data, they establish property values and forecast returns for every state, ZIP Code, block, and home in the US. Insights are delivered via app or API.

Personas

To our partners at CoreLogic it was imperative that we designed an application that was simple enough for casual real estate investors to use without being overwhelmed or intimidated, but robust enough so that professional real estate investors can quickly and reliably find properties that match their investment criteria. Because of this our user personas focus heavily on these two type of investors.

Low-Fidelity

Now equipped with knowledge about our competitors, an understanding of our primary user base, and a game plan from our partners at CoreLogic, it was time to start designing. First and foremost, I started off by creating some simple wireframes to flesh out our initial ideas and give us a blueprint to build off of going foreword.

High-Fidelity

Next, I cooked up some high-fidelity mockups that doubled as a clickable prototype thanks to the power of Figma. The value of having a high-fidelity prototype here was immeasurable because it allowed us to confirm that the application satisfied our primary stakeholders (CoreLogic and real estate investors) without even having to write a single line of code. Feel free to check out the prototype for yourself if you would like.

Demo

Key Features: Advanced Search & Saving Search Criteria

Through interviewing investors and chatting with people at CoreLogic, we discovered that most real estate investors have specific formulas they follow for determining worthwhile properties. Their “secret sauce” as one investor described it to us. Therefore, I felt it was super important to include a feature that allows users to save specific combinations of search filters. This allows investors to quickly search for properties that match their specific formulas without having to play around with the filters each and every time they log onto the site. Overall, a nice shortcut to have in place for experienced users.

Key Features: Bookmarking Properties

Another very important feature I felt the need to include was a bookmarking ability. If users find a property they are interested in but aren’t ready to pull the trigger on just yet, they can bookmark it for later. Without this feature I can only imagine how much of a pain it would be to track down a specific property again after you’ve left the application and come back some time later.

Style

As far as the style of the widget goes, I aimed to keep the overall layout and colors consistent with CoreLogic’s other services while also throwing in dashes of my own personal touch here and there.

Takeaways

CoreLogic was a joy to work with. There was great chemistry amongst the team all around which enabled us to communicate well with each other so that we were able to build them exactly what they wanted. They were great at being supportive of what were building while also providing some guidance through constructive criticism when we would showcase our progress during meetings at their office. Furthermore, it was immensely satisfying to be able to bring together all the skills I’ve been fostering during my 4 years here at UC Irvine to build a product that I’m proud of for a real client.
